Apex vs Topstep vs Bulenox

A clean, no-BS rule comparison so you can see exactly which futures prop firm fits your trading plan.

1. Trailing Drawdown Rules (Most Important Difference)

Trailing drawdown rules are the #1 reason traders pass or fail. Study this section with Trailing Drawdown Explained.

FirmTrailing Drawdown TypeStops Trailing at?
ApexTrailing (closest to price)Account hits starting balance + drawdown
BulenoxTrailingSame as Apex
TopstepTrailing, BUT only on closed balanceNever trails unrealized

Topstep’s “closed PnL only” rule is easier for beginners. Apex and Bulenox require tighter stops and more discipline.

2. Profit Targets

FirmTypical Profit Target ($50k eval)
Apex$3,000
Topstep$3,000
Bulenox$3,000

All three are basically identical on profit targets.

3. Daily Loss Limits

FirmDaily Loss Limit ($50k eval)
Apex~$1,100
Topstep$1,000
Bulenox~$1,000

These numbers plug directly into your Daily Loss & Reset Rules.

4. Scaling Rules (During the Evaluation)

FirmScaling
ApexNo scaling rules — trade max size allowed
BulenoxNo scaling rules
TopstepStrict scaling rules (contract limits per profit level)

If you follow the One-Contract Strategy, scaling doesn't matter anyway.

5. Evaluation Structure

FirmSteps Required
Apex1 step
Bulenox1 step
Topstep2 steps (Stage 1 & Stage 2)

Apex/Bulenox = faster. Topstep = slower but cleaner risk rules.

6. Payout Structure

FirmPayout Rules
ApexKeep 100% up to $25k, then 90%
BulenoxSimilar to Apex (varies per account)
TopstepKeep 100% of your first $5k, then 90%

7. Difficulty Rating (Practical, Not Marketing BS)

  • Easiest: Topstep (closed PnL trailing)
  • Medium: Apex
  • Hardest: Bulenox (strictest trailing behavior)

This matches the experience described in Overtrading in Evaluations.

8. Which Should You Choose?

If you want fastest / cheapest:

Apex

If you want easiest rules:

Topstep

If you want highest profit split + frequent promos:

Apex or Bulenox
