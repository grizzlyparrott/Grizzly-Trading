HG Tick Size, Tick Value, and Margin: The Numbers That Actually Matter

Most traders screw up copper futures (HG) because they don’t respect the tick size, tick value, or margin demands. HG isn’t complicated, but the contract punishes anyone who underestimates how fast dollars move per tick.

The Exact HG Tick Size

HG trades in increments of $0.0005 per pound. That looks tiny, which is why beginners oversize the contract and get blown out. Copper doesn’t need big decimal moves to put real money on the line.

  • Minimum tick: $0.0005
  • Contract size: 25,000 pounds
  • Ticks per cent: 20

One full cent ($0.01) equals 20 ticks — and 20 ticks adds up fast during strong macro impulses or trend continuation days.

HG Tick Value: What Every Tick Actually Costs

Every tick in HG is worth $12.50. Traders new to metals massively underestimate that number because the decimals look “small.” They’re not.

Tick Value Example

  • HG moves from 3.7500 → 3.7525
  • That’s 5 ticks
  • 5 × $12.50 = $62.50

If you think 10–20 ticks “isn’t much,” you’re already done. Copper regularly pushes 60–120 ticks on strong data releases.

HG Margin Requirements

Margins shift with volatility, but the basic idea stays the same: HG isn’t a cheap contract. The CME structures it for industrial hedgers and macro funds, not undercapitalized tourists.

  • Initial Margin (approx): Varies with volatility
  • Maintenance Margin: Slightly lower than initial
  • Day Trading Margin: Broker-specific and usually misleadingly low

Day trading margins from brokers make traders think HG is lightweight. The contract doesn’t care. If you get steamrolled by a $500–$1,500 move, the broker will liquidate you instantly.

How Tick Value Impacts Position Sizing

Use the tick value to size positions, not your feelings. Copper doesn’t behave like ES or CL. It trends in clean, heavy swings and you need to size accordingly.

  • Know your max dollar-per-tick tolerance
  • Size based on distance to invalidation
  • Expect multi-tick slippage during data releases

If you’ve read Article #1 in the copper series, you already know why HG structure and macro demand make these swings predictable but expensive.

HG Price Behavior by Tick Mechanics

Because HG trades in such granular increments, its microstructure is smoother than metals like SI. But don’t confuse smooth with safe — copper can run a full cent ($250) without blinking during risk-on expansions.

Common Behavior Patterns

  • Copper drifts cleanly during Asia
  • Europe adds direction
  • US session injects volatility
  • Inventory data can blow out both sides

This makes ticks meaningful — every increment carries weight because HG rarely “chops” without purpose.

Final Takeaways

The HG tick size and tick value define how much risk you’re taking on every trade. Copper’s $12.50 tick value forces discipline, and the margin structure prevents careless oversizing. If you don’t size correctly, HG will clean you out fast. Master the numbers and the contract becomes one of the cleanest macro metals to trade.
