6B Contract Specs, Tick Size, and Margin Requirements Explained

If you don’t know the exact specs of 6B British Pound futures, you’re guessing your risk. This contract is simple once you understand how each number affects your position size and how fast the market can move against you.

6B Contract Specifications

Here’s what you’re trading when you trade a single 6B contract:

Spec Value
Contract Size £62,500
Minimum Tick 0.0001
Tick Value $6.25 per tick
Trading Hours (CME Globex) Sun–Fri, nearly 24 hours

This setup is similar to the rhythm described in market liquidity cycles.

Tick Size and Why It Matters

Every 0.0001 move = $6.25. A full 0.0100 shift in price = 100 ticks = $625. On BOE or CPI days, you can see 6B jump 80–120 ticks in under a minute. If your sizing is sloppy, you’ll get steamrolled instantly.

Initial Margin vs Maintenance Margin

The CME sets baseline margins, but brokers and prop firms tend to adjust these numbers depending on volatility.

  • Initial margin — upfront capital required to enter
  • Maintenance margin — minimum balance you must keep

Prop firms may treat these differently, which ties into your existing article margin policy differences.

How to Size Positions in 6B

The math is straightforward:

  • 1 tick = $6.25
  • 10 ticks = $62.50
  • 20 ticks = $125
  • 40 ticks = $250

Most clean intraday moves run 20–40 ticks before pulling back. If a 40-tick stop feels too large for you, you’re trading too big.

6B Contracts Have Clear Specs

The 6B market features a £62,500 contract size, $6.25 tick value, and moderate margin requirements. These parameters define how the market behaves and how price moves within its structure.
