Analyzing Weekly Trends and Swings in 6B Futures

Most traders only look at intraday charts and never zoom out. Weekly structure in 6B shows you the real direction. When the weekly trend aligns with your intraday bias, trades run further with less stress. When you fight the weekly trend, you lose more often than not.

Why Weekly Structure Matters

The weekly chart filters out the noise and exposes the major swings driving 6B:

  • Institutional positioning
  • Multi-week momentum cycles
  • Major support/resistance
  • True swing highs and lows

This ties directly to the big-picture concepts in Market Structure Basics: The Framework Behind Every Price Move.

How to Identify Weekly Trend Direction

Weekly trend is defined by:

  • Higher highs + higher lows → uptrend
  • Lower highs + lower lows → downtrend
  • Flat highs + flat lows → range

If this conflicts with your intraday setup, you’re trading into a wall.

Common Weekly Swing Patterns in 6B

  • Two-week continuation — strong momentum after a breakout
  • Wick rejections at major levels — early signs of reversal
  • Slow grind trends — 6B’s most reliable long-term moves

These patterns align with the momentum behavior in Market Volatility Cycles: How Volatility Expands and Contracts.

How Weekly Levels Control Intraday Action

Intraday price constantly respects weekly structure:

  • Weekly highs act as breakout magnets
  • Weekly lows create massive liquidity pools
  • Mid-range often becomes a rotation target

Using Weekly Swings to Improve Entry Quality

When the weekly trend is bullish:

  • Long setups become higher probability
  • Shorts should be taken aggressively or avoided
  • P ullbacks into weekly support offer the cleanest opportunities

When weekly trend is bearish, flip the logic.

Table: Weekly Trend Alignment vs Trade Quality

Trend Alignment Trade Quality
Same direction High probability, strong follow-through
Against trend Lower win rate, weaker moves
Weekly range Choppy behavior, scalp environment

Final Thoughts

If you’re not checking the weekly trend before trading 6B, you’re operating without the bigger picture. Weekly swings show the real direction, major liquidity zones, and where intraday setups will actually follow through.
