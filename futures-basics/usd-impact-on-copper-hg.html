How the U.S. Dollar Impacts Copper Futures: HG Price Relationship Explained

Copper futures (HG) have a tight inverse relationship with the U.S. dollar. When the dollar strengthens, HG usually drops. When the dollar weakens, HG typically rallies. This isn’t optional knowledge — it’s mandatory if you want to understand copper trend behavior.

Why the Dollar Matters for Copper Prices

Copper is priced globally in dollars. When the dollar strengthens, copper becomes more expensive for foreign buyers. That reduces demand and pressures HG lower.

  • Stronger USD = weaker copper demand
  • Weaker USD = stronger copper demand
  • Sharp USD moves = immediate HG volatility

This creates one of the most reliable cross-asset correlations in futures.

The Dollar–Copper Inverse Correlation Pattern

HG doesn’t mirror the dollar tick-for-tick, but major moves in DXY drive multi-session copper trends.

When the USD strengthens

  • Importers slow copper purchases
  • Speculators unwind long positions
  • HG trends downward smoothly

When the USD weakens

  • International copper demand increases
  • Commodity funds rotate into metals
  • HG rallies in extended legs

This relationship sits on top of the supply chain behavior explained in Article #3.

How Macro Data Affects the USD–HG Connection

Copper reacts to USD movement triggered by macroeconomic events. The stronger the macro catalyst, the more violent the HG reaction.

  • FOMC meetings: Dollar volatility → copper volatility
  • Inflation prints (CPI/PPI): Dollar spikes → HG reversal setups
  • Jobs data: Strong jobs → stronger USD → weaker HG
  • GDP data: Growth prints shift both markets at once

HG often lags the initial USD spike, creating high-probability continuation trades.

When Copper Ignores the Dollar

Sometimes HG diverges from the USD correlation. This only happens when supply-chain forces overpower currency effects.

Copper ignores USD during:

  • Major LME/COMEX inventory drawdowns
  • China stimulus waves
  • Mining disruptions
  • Global infrastructure booms

These are the situations discussed in Article #5 where copper trades on pure fundamentals.

How to Use USD Trends in HG Trading

The USD gives you bias, context, and timing. It won’t tell you entries, but it does tell you which direction has the wind at its back.

  • Trade long HG when the dollar is weakening
  • Trade short HG when the dollar is strengthening
  • Expect volatility during USD macro events
  • Confirm with inventory and China trends

HG is at its cleanest when USD and industrial data move in the same direction.

Final Takeaways

The U.S. dollar is one of the most important drivers of copper futures. Dollar strength weakens copper, dollar weakness fuels rallies, and macro data has the power to reverse HG intraday. Track USD trends along with inventories and Chinese demand to understand copper’s full directional picture.
