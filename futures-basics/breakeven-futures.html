Break-Even Math for Futures Traders

What Break-Even Actually Means

Break-even in futures isn’t theory. It’s a number: the exact tick distance you need to cover your commissions and not lose money. Every contract has its own tick value, so the math is simple—no guesswork.

The Three Inputs

  • Tick value: how much each tick is worth.
  • Commission per round turn: what your broker takes.
  • Your entry price vs exit: how many ticks you gained or lost.

Formula

Break-even ticks = Commission ÷ Tick Value

Round up. You can’t gain fractional ticks.

Examples (Real Numbers)

MES (Micro S&P 500)

Tick value: $1.25 Commission: $1.20 per round turn Break-even: 1.20 ÷ 1.25 = 0.96 → 1 tick

MNQ (Micro Nasdaq)

Tick value: $0.50 Commission: $1.20 Break-even: 1.20 ÷ 0.50 = 2.4 → 3 ticks

GC (Gold Futures)

Tick value: $10 Commission: $2.50 Break-even: 2.50 ÷ 10 = 0.25 → 1 tick

Why This Matters

If you scalp, this is life or death. If your strategy targets 2 ticks but your break-even is 3 ticks, you lose long-term. If your target is bigger than your break-even number, the math is on your side.

Know your break-even number before you take the trade. If your target is smaller, the math will eat you alive. No strategy beats basic arithmetic.
