How BOJ Intervention Shows Up on the 6J Chart

When the Bank of Japan intervenes in the currency market, 6J futures react instantly. These aren’t normal moves — they’re vertical, violent, and unmistakable if you know what to look for. Intervention leaves a signature on the chart that no other event can replicate.

1. The Initial Vertical Spike

The first sign is a massive candle that looks completely out of place relative to the rest of the session.

  • zero pullback
  • straight-line movement
  • volume explosion

This isn’t “momentum.” It’s the BOJ slamming billions into the market to defend the yen, usually after extreme USDJPY levels.

2. DOM and Volume Behavior Goes Insane

The DOM thins, then fills, then vanishes — all in seconds. Volume prints blocks far larger than any institutional trader normally uses.

  • size appears mid-range instantly
  • market orders sweep multiple levels
  • algos shut off because the tape breaks correlation

If you’ve studied liquidity void behavior, intervention basically creates the biggest void possible.

3. Correlations Break Immediately

During intervention:

  • U.S. yields temporarily stop mattering
  • ES/NQ correlations break
  • USDJPY disconnects from normal levels

Everything you know from 6J–yield dynamics pauses for a few minutes.

4. The Follow-Through Move Is Always One-Sided

Intervention isn’t subtle. It forces price to move. You’ll see:

  • an initial ripping candle
  • small retrace (if any)
  • a second impulse wave

This two-stage pattern is one of the most reliable signatures of BOJ action.

5. When It Usually Happens

BOJ intervention almost always hits during:

  • Tokyo hours
  • Tokyo–Singapore overlap
  • major USDJPY “line in the sand” levels

These windows align with the volatility behavior outlined in your volatility article.

6. The Warning Signs Before Intervention Hits

Smart desks catch intervention hints early. Here’s what they watch:

  • USDJPY approaching historic “danger levels”
  • BOJ officials making sudden public comments
  • news agencies reporting “BOJ watching FX closely”
  • options market pricing huge IV jumps

When these line up, liquidity dries just before the BOJ hits the button.

BOJ Intervention Leaves Distinct Market Signals

Spikes, broken correlations, extreme volume, and extended one-direction moves mark intervention events in 6J. Recognizing these patterns provides context for unusual price behavior without implying prediction or guaranteed outcomes.
