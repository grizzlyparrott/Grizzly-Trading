Trading Sessions Explained

Trading sessions shape volatility and volume. If you don’t know which session you’re trading in, you’ll constantly be confused by random price action that isn’t random at all. Each session has its own personality and its own traps.

The Three Major Sessions

The global market runs in three major phases:

  • Asia Session
  • London Session
  • New York Session

Each one shifts who’s active, how fast price moves, and what liquidity looks like. If you already know how market imbalance works, this is when imbalance becomes predictable.

Asia Session (Low Liquidity, Slow Movement)

Asia is the calmest session. Liquidity is lighter, volatility is lower, and the market mostly ranges. Breakouts usually fail unless they’re driven by news.

Trait Asia Session Behavior
Liquidity Low
Volatility Controlled, slow
Typical Price Action Range-bound, smaller rotations

London Session (Volatility Kicks In)

London is where the real movement often starts. Institutions in Europe step in, liquidity improves, and volatility spikes. Trends frequently begin here.

  • bigger rotations
  • more meaningful breakouts
  • faster tape

London also creates the imbalances that New York later reacts to. That ties directly into price discovery.

New York Session (Highest Volume, Strongest Moves)

New York dominates the day. Most major news releases drop here, and the U.S. session often decides the final direction.

Trait New York Behavior
Liquidity Highest of all sessions
Volatility Fast, aggressive
Price Action Strong trends or violent reversals

Session Overlaps (Where Things Get Wild)

The most important part of the global day is when sessions overlap:

  • London–New York overlap — highest liquidity and fastest moves of the day
  • Asia–London overlap — usually slower, but can create the initial setup

Ignore Session Context, and You’ll Keep Misreading the Tape

Different traders bring different agendas. Treat every session the same, and you’ll chase moves that were never meant to last. Learn who’s in control, or keep getting faked out by action that isn’t yours to trade.
