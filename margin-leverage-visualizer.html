Futures Margin & Leverage Visualizer

Pick a contract, plug in your account size and margin, and drag the slider. See exactly how much leverage you are actually running and what a move in price does to your account.

Inputs

Enter price exactly how your platform shows it.
Index / metals / energies: normal price (e.g. 5300.00, 78.25, 2450.4).
FX (6E, 6B, etc.): decimal (e.g. 1.1050, 0.7500).
Grains / softs: dollars per unit (e.g. 5.25 for ZC, 0.85 for ZL, 190.00 for KC).

Positive = move in your favor. Negative = move against you.
This is in price units, not ticks. For example: 0.0100 on 6E, 0.25 on ZC, 5.00 on CL.

Results

Fill out the inputs and hit Calculate.

How To Read This

Notional & Leverage

Notional = price × contract multiplier × contracts. Leverage = total notional ÷ your account. If you are running 30x leverage on a tiny account, you know how that ends.

Margin Usage

Margin used = margin/contract × contracts. Margin % tells you how much of your account is locked just to hold the position.

P/L Per 1.00 Move

Every 1.00 move in price is a fixed dollar amount per contract for that product. The slider shows what a move of X units does in real dollars and % of your account.
