NinjaTrader 8 DOM Basics

The SuperDOM in NinjaTrader 8 is built for fast futures execution. If you scalp or if you care about speed, depth of market, or ladder-style entry, this is the tool you use. Chart Trader is fine for slower entries, but the DOM is the fastest way to get in and out without clicking across your chart like a tourist.

Opening the SuperDOM

From the Control Center:

  1. New → SuperDOM (Dynamic)
  2. Select your instrument (ES, MNQ, CL, etc.)

The DOM loads instantly. If nothing is moving, you're either disconnected or staring at an expired contract.

Understanding the DOM Columns

The NT8 DOM has a simple structure:

ColumnPurpose
Bid ColumnLimit buy orders stacked by price
Price LadderThe price scale; each row is one tick
Ask ColumnLimit sell orders stacked by price
Order Entry ColumnsClick levels for limit, stop, and market orders

The DOM shows liquidity and price action without distractions. This is why scalpers prefer it.

Placing Orders on the DOM

Order entry is simple:

  • Left-click below price → Buy Limit
  • Left-click above price → Sell Limit
  • Right-click above price → Buy Stop
  • Right-click below price → Sell Stop

Market orders can be placed using the buttons on the left side panel.

Flatten, Reverse, and Close

The NT8 DOM gives you hard-stop controls:

  • Flatten: closes the position and cancels all orders
  • Reverse: flips your position instantly
  • Close: closes position but leaves working orders (rarely used)

If you're in trouble and need out now, Flatten is the button you hit.

Reading Depth of Market (DOM Depth)

Depth shows how many contracts sit at each price level:

  • Numbers on the bid = buyers stacked below price
  • Numbers on the ask = sellers stacked above price

This is NOT future prediction. It's liquidity, not direction. But it helps you see:

  • Where liquidity might absorb price
  • Where thin spots could slip fast
  • Where large players might be stacking

Don't worship DOM depth—just learn to read it.

Attaching ATM Strategies

At the top of the DOM, you select your ATM strategy:

  • Choose an existing ATM
  • Build a new ATM on the fly
  • Attach stops and targets automatically

If you're trading a prop firm evaluation, this is mandatory. No manual stops unless you enjoy gambling.

Order Modifications on the DOM

Once your bracket is live:

  • Drag your stop up/down
  • Drag your target up/down
  • Shift-drag to modify size

Everything updates instantly. The DOM is faster than chart modifications.

Common Beginner Mistakes on the DOM

  • Placing a buy limit above price (that’s a market order)
  • Forgetting ATM is set to “None”
  • Wrong account selected at the top
  • Trading the wrong contract month
  • Flattening while orders are still pending—causing accidental reversals

Check your account, ATM, and instrument every time before clicking anything.

The DOM Is Where Precision Lives

NinjaTrader 8’s DOM is built for speed, but it pays off even if you’re not scalping. Learning it sharpens your execution, trims your mistakes, and gives you real control over order placement. Fast or slow, if you want tighter entries, the DOM needs to be part of your skillset.
